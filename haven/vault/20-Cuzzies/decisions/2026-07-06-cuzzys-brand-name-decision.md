# Decision: Cuzzy's white-label brand keeps the Cuzzie's name

**Date:** 2026-07-06
**Owner:** Lemar

## What was decided
Option A — keep the Cuzzie's name on the white-label product line, rather than
building a new/separate identity or a hybrid (new consumer name + Cuzzie's kept
as internal origin story). Lemar reacted ✅ on Option A in the #decisions card
(thread ts 1783372386.951919, option reply ts 1783372391.176029) posted during
the 2026-07-06 17:04 ET scan.

## Why (the reasoning that must survive)
The card framed this as Q2 of the 100-question brand brief: Cuzzie's carries
genuine local recognition in Camden and the Josh/Cuzzie's founder story, but
the dispensary has soured several cannabis vendor/cultivator relationships by
falling behind on payments — a risk the option explicitly flagged (vendors and
cultivators still owed money may associate the name with the unpaid debts,
working against the new supply partnerships the white-label model depends on).
Lemar picked to keep the name anyway; the reasoning behind accepting that risk
was not stated in-thread beyond the pick itself.

## Alternatives considered
- Option B — new, separate brand identity (clean slate, no AP baggage with
  suppliers, but forfeits existing local recognition and starts brand
  awareness from zero).
- Option C — hybrid: new consumer-facing name for the white-label line, with
  Cuzzie's kept as the internal origin story, surfaced later once vendor
  relationships are repaired.

## Consequences / follow-ups
This choice steers packaging, trademark filing, and the rest of the
brand-identity thread (Q1, Q3, Q9, etc. of the 100-question brief are
downstream of it) — see
`haven/vault/40-Projects/cuzzys-brand/2026-07-05-cuzzys-white-label-brand-questions.md`.
The vendor/cultivator-debt risk the option itself flagged is still open and
tracked separately on the #on-button Reopen Checklist (unpaid AP list).
