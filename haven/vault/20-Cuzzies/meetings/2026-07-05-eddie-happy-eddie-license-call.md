## Call script outline

**Opening**
"Hey Eddie, thanks for making time. Wanted to pick up where we left off — happy to
dive into the license/ops side first, then I've got a second idea I'd love your take on."

**Track 1 — License/operations transition (equity sale / buyer angle)**
1. Ask who the interested investors are — individuals, a fund, or an existing operator
   looking to expand into Camden/South Jersey?
2. Ask what "transition" would look like for them: buying the CRC license + lease
   position outright, or an operating partnership/management deal?
3. Share where things actually stand, plainly: lease is terminated with an active
   landlord-tenant case (trial 7/30/2026, arrears ~$42,157.37); a license transfer would
   need CRC approval and likely needs the lease/landlord situation resolved or assumed
   by the buyer as part of the deal.
4. Ask about timeline and rough deal size/structure they typically look for — no need to
   negotiate on this call, just gauge fit and next step (e.g., NDA, intro call with their
   investor, sharing high-level financials).
5. Close: if there's real interest, propose adding them to the existing lender/investor
   package review process (same one used for Cuzzie's capital raise) rather than a
   separate one-off deal.

**Track 2 — White-label / foot-in-the-door angle**
1. Ask Eddie about the NJ pop-up/meet-and-greet expansion he mentioned — what does
   Happy Eddie need from a local partner or host site?
2. Raise the idea directly: is there a path for Cuzzie's (or a successor entity) to be a
   white-label or private-label partner for Happy Eddie in NJ — either producing/packing
   under the Happy Eddie brand, or hosting Happy Eddie pop-up activations as Cuzzie's
   pivots business models?
3. Ask what a "foot in the door" would realistically look like at this stage — a small
   pilot, a hosted event, a referral into their brand network — versus something that
   needs the dispensary to be operating again first.
4. Close: ask for next step regardless of Track 1's outcome — even if the license/ops
   conversation goes nowhere, is the white-label/pop-up conversation worth continuing on
   its own?

**Wrap-up**
"Really appreciate you being straight with me on both of these — let's stay in touch on
whichever piece moves first." Note anything Eddie commits to and follow up by email.
