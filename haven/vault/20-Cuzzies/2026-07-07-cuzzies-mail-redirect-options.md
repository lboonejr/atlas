# Where to get Cuzzie's mail while temporarily closed

Lemar asked where to get Cuzzie's mail since the store closed and nothing is being
delivered to 2750 Mount Ephraim Ave, Camden.

## Options
1. **USPS Change of Address (temporary business forward)** — forward all mail from
   the Camden address to a reachable address (home, or Josh's) for up to 12 months,
   extendable. Best fit for an open-ended closure — captures everything automatically,
   including legal/CRC mail. Set up at usps.com/move or any post office.
2. **USPS Hold Mail** — only holds up to 30 days, then mail resumes delivery to the
   (closed) store. Not a real fix for a multi-month closure, but fine as a stopgap
   while the Change of Address takes effect.
3. **PO Box** near Camden — mail goes to a box Lemar/Josh can check directly; doesn't
   require senders to know a new address, but Cuzzie's would need to update its
   address on file with vendors/banks/CRC to actually reach it (mail addressed to the
   store street address won't auto-redirect without also filing #1).
4. **Directly notify key senders** (CRC, bank, insurers, high-priority vendors) of an
   alternate mailing address — highest-signal but manual, worth doing regardless of
   which of 1–3 is chosen, since legal/regulatory mail (like the CRC thread already in
   motion) is the highest-stakes category.

## Recommendation
Combine #1 (USPS forward to a reachable address) with #4 for CRC and the bank —
covers everything automatically and makes sure the highest-stakes senders don't rely
on the forward alone.
