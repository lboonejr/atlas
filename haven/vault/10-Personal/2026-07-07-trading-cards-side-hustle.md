# Trading cards buy/sell side hustle — starter framework

Lemar's idea: get into buying and selling trading cards as another side project.
Wants to start with the basics — what makes a card valuable, and where people buy
and sell.

## What makes a card valuable
- **Rarity / print run** — limited parallels, short-printed rookies, error cards.
- **Condition & grading** — PSA, BGS, and SGC are the three main graders; a PSA 10
  vs. a raw/played copy of the same card can be a 10x+ price gap. Grading costs
  ~$15–$50/card depending on turnaround tier.
- **Player/set popularity** — rookie cards of stars, iconic sets (vintage, first-year
  releases) hold value best; hype-driven cards (new draft picks) are volatile.
- **Demand cycles** — sports cards spike around playoffs/draft season; Pokémon/TCG
  spikes around new set releases.

## Where to buy/sell
- **eBay** — biggest marketplace, best for singles and grading arbitrage (buy raw,
  grade, resell).
- **COMC / TCGplayer** — consignment-style, lower-touch selling for singles.
- **Whatnot** — live-auction/breaks format, growing fast, good for volume moves.
- **Local card shops & shows** — best for in-person deals, gut-checks on value, and
  building relationships with buyers.

## Suggested starting steps
1. Pick a lane first (sports vs. Pokémon/TCG/other) — different buyer pools, different
   grading economics.
2. Start with a small test budget ($200–500), buy a handful of cards you understand
   the value of, track cost/grading/sale price to learn the margins before scaling.
3. Learn to spot centering/edges/corners/surface issues before submitting anything
   for grading — bad submissions waste grading fees.
4. Track inventory + P&L from day one (a simple spreadsheet or an app like Collectr).

## Decision point
Sports cards or another category (Pokémon/other TCG) first — which fits Lemar's
existing knowledge/interest better?
