# 1991 Lexus LS400 — friend deal, $3,000–3,500, 230k miles

Lemar's friend (who flips cars) is working on a 1991 Lexus LS400, 230k miles,
asking $3,000–$3,500 depending on repairs. Wants it assessed for the car search.

## Quick assessment
- The 1UZ-FE V8 in the first-gen LS400 is one of the most reliable engines Toyota/
  Lexus ever built — 230k–300k+ miles on the original engine/transmission isn't
  unusual with consistent maintenance.
- At 230k, the things actually worth checking: cooling system (radiator/hoses — a
  common failure point that can cook the engine if ignored), suspension bushings/
  shocks (35-year-old rubber), and rust (NJ road salt is hard on the rocker
  panels/subframe on cars this old).
- $3,000–3,500 for a 230k-mile, 35-year-old car is reasonable *if* it's mechanically
  sorted — on the higher side if it still needs real work, since parts/body condition
  matter more than mileage on a car this age.
- Since the seller is a friend who works on cars, worth asking directly: what's
  already been fixed vs. what he was mid-repair on, whether there's a paper trail
  (receipts), and the actual title/mileage status (a 1991 car may be exempt from
  odometer disclosure in NJ — worth confirming, not assuming).

## Recommendation
Worth a look given the reliability reputation, but get the repair list + a look at
the car in person (or a trusted second set of eyes) before committing, same as any
other car-search candidate.
