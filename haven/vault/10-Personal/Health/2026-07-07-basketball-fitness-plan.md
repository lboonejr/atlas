# Back-to-basketball plan — bodyweight, by October

Lemar wants to get back into basketball shape before October, starting from
out-of-shape, using bodyweight-only strength and conditioning to start.

## 12-week bodyweight plan (roughly now → early October)

**Weeks 1–4 — Foundation (3 days/week)**
- Mobility warm-up every session (hips, ankles, shoulders — 5–10 min).
- Bodyweight strength circuit: squats, walking lunges, push-ups, planks, glute
  bridges — 3 rounds, moderate reps, focus on form over speed.
- Light cardio 2x/week (brisk walk/jog, 20–25 min) to rebuild a base.

**Weeks 5–9 — Conditioning (3–4 days/week)**
- Add plyometrics: box-step jumps or broad jumps, jump squats — low volume at
  first, form first (impact is the injury risk at this stage).
- Agility work: ladder drills or cone shuffles, defensive slide drills.
- Interval cardio: 20–30 min sessions mixing hard/easy (e.g., 1 min hard / 2 min
  easy) to build basketball-specific wind.
- Keep the strength circuit from weeks 1–4, add reps/rounds as it gets easier.

**Weeks 10–12 — Basketball-specific (4 days/week)**
- Ball-handling drills + light shooting, building toward pickup/1-on-1.
- Vertical jump work (depth jumps, single-leg bounds) 1–2x/week.
- Full-court conditioning: sprint intervals mimicking game bursts.
- One light pickup or scrimmage session by week 11–12 to test readiness before
  October.

## Notes
- Bodyweight-only fits the "starting from scratch, safely" ask — no equipment
  needed for weeks 1–4; can add a jump rope/resistance band later if useful.
- Rest at least 1 day between strength sessions early on; soreness/joint pain,
  not just fatigue, is the signal to back off.
