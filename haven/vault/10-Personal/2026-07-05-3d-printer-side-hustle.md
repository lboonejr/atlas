# 3D printer side hustle — cannabis accessories (and "anything")

Lemar dropped an idea in #atlas (2026-07-05 15:23 ET): investing in a 3D printer
soon to make cannabis accessories — and possibly other products — as a side
hustle. Asked for help fleshing it out.

## Probe posted in-thread (2026-07-05 16:06 ET)
Five sharpening questions were posted back to him in the #atlas thread, still
unanswered as of this note:
1. Budget — entry FDM printer ~$200–500 vs. resin printer (finer detail — grinders,
   trays, stash boxes) ~$300–700 + resin/ventilation. What's the ceiling?
2. First products — cannabis accessories specifically (grinder, rolling tray,
   stash box, ashtray) vs. the wide-open "anything" catalog. 2–3 first keeps it
   from sprawling.
3. Where it sells — Etsy/craft fairs vs. through Cuzzie's/Station's own customer
   base once reopened (different rules for branded accessories through a
   licensed dispensary channel vs. general retail).
4. Time — how much hands-on time vs. eventually handing it off, on top of the
   wind-down + court matter + comedy club already on his plate.
5. Branding — separate personal brand, or tied to Cuzzie's name/logo?

Samira's gut lean, offered in the same reply: start small — one entry FDM
printer, 1–2 simple designs, sell on Etsy/local markets first as a cheap
experiment before any bigger spend.

## Update 2026-07-05 (17:40 ET) — Lemar's answers
1. Budget: ~$500 ceiling, all-in.
2. First products: not locked yet — leaning custom lighter covers/ashtrays or
   dispensary logo keychains.
3. Channel: Etsy first, to see what actually sells, then filter the winners into
   The Station's own customer base.
4. Time: 6 weeks of setup/hands-on, then hand the ongoing operation off to Arianna.
5. Branding: separate brand, not tied to Cuzzie's or The Station.

### Plan, given the above
At a $500 all-in ceiling with lighter covers/ashtrays/keychains as the likely
first products, an FDM printer (not resin) is the right call — plenty of detail
for these shapes, cheaper consumables, no ventilation/resin-handling overhead.
Budget split: ~$250–350 printer (e.g. Bambu Lab A1 mini, Creality K1C, Elegoo
Neptune 4 — all well-reviewed entry/mid FDM), ~$50–100 filament (PLA to start),
~$50–100 buffer for a first-set of finishing supplies (sandpaper, spray primer/
paint if the designs need color).

6-week setup timeline:
- Week 1: printer arrives, calibrate, print a few test/calibration objects.
- Week 2: design or source 2–3 candidate designs (keychain first — smallest,
  fastest, cheapest to iterate; lighter cover/ashtray second).
- Week 3: print small test batches (5–10 units each design), check fit/finish/
  print time and per-unit material cost.
- Week 4: pick the winning design(s), open the Etsy shop, list with real photos.
- Week 5: first sales push (socials, maybe a Station/Cuzzie's community mention
  once separate-brand positioning is set), watch what actually sells.
- Week 6: document the repeatable process (print settings, packaging, listing
  template) so Arianna can run it without Lemar day-to-day.

Open questions for whenever Lemar wants to move forward: does "separate brand"
need a name/logo before the Etsy shop opens, and does Arianna know this handoff
is coming / has she agreed to take it on?
