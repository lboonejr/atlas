# 3D printer side hustle — making cannabis accessories (and more)

Lemar captured in #atlas (2026-07-05 15:23 ET): a random idea about investing in a
3D printer soon to make cannabis accessories — and "anything to be honest" — as a
possible side hustle. Asked to flesh it out.

## Situation
Idea-stage only — no printer bought, no product line picked, no budget set. This
would sit alongside (not replace) the Cuzzie's wind-down / reopening work and the
comedy-club side project already in motion.

## What would sharpen this
1. **Budget** — what's the printer spend ceiling? (Entry FDM printers run
   ~$200–500; resin printers for fine detail — grinders, trays, stash boxes — run
   ~$300–700 plus resin/ventilation costs.)
2. **First products** — cannabis accessories specifically (grinders, rolling
   trays, stash boxes, ashtrays, joint holders) vs. the broader "anything" catalog?
   Picking 2–3 first items keeps this from sprawling.
3. **Where it sells** — Etsy/online, local markets/craft fairs, or through
   Cuzzie's/Station's own customer base once reopened? (Selling cannabis-branded
   accessories through a licensed dispensary channel vs. general retail has
   different rules worth checking.)
4. **Time** — how much of this is Lemar's own time vs. something to eventually
   hand off, given Cuzzie's/Station/court matter/comedy club already on the plate?
5. **IP/legal** — any concern about branding pieces with Cuzzie's name/logo vs.
   keeping this a fully separate personal brand?

## Options
- **A. Start small** — one entry-level FDM printer, one or two simple designs
  (grinder card, rolling tray), sell on Etsy/local markets first, treat it as a
  cheap experiment before any bigger spend.
- **B. Go all-in on quality** — resin printer for fine detail work aimed at a
  premium cannabis-accessory line, higher upfront cost, slower to first sale.
- **C. Park it** — idea logged, revisit once Cuzzie's/Station wind-down work has
  more runway.

## Recommendation
Option A — lowest cost, fastest to a real first sale, and doesn't compete for
attention with the wind-down work happening right now. Once there's a first
product actually selling, it's easy to decide whether to reinvest in a better
printer.

## Decision needed
Budget ceiling + which 2–3 products to prototype first — reply in #atlas (or
here) and this gets built out further; too open-ended to spec next steps without
those two answers.
